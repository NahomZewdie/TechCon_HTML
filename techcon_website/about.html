History

This annual conference, focused on high-voltage maintenance programs and asset management strategies for the electrical grid, has been consistently held since 1996. It brings together utility and power generation experts.

Mission

The conference operates on several key goals and driving principles: Enhancing Grid Reliability and Resiliency: A primary objective is to equip utility and power generation personnel with the knowledge and tools necessary to ensure the continuous and stable operation of the electrical grid, addressing challenges posed by aging infrastructure and increasing demands. Promoting Best Practices in Maintenance and Asset Management: TechCon is dedicated to sharing cutting-edge techniques, technologies, and methodologies for condition monitoring, diagnostic testing, and maintenance strategies. This includes discussions on predictive maintenance, risk-based asset management, and life-extension programs for critical assets. Fostering Knowledge Exchange and Collaboration: The conference serves as a vital platform for utility and power generation experts, field technicians, engineers, and suppliers to exchange experiences, discuss emerging issues, and collaborate on solutions to common industry challenges. It facilitates networking opportunities that lead to practical improvements. Advancing Technical Expertise: Through detailed technical presentations, case studies, and sometimes hands-on workshops, TechCon aims to deepen the technical understanding of attendees in areas such as transformer maintenance, circuit breaker testing, substation diagnostics, and switchgear operation. Integrating New Technologies and Innovations: A core principle is to showcase and discuss the latest technological advancements, including new diagnostic equipment, software solutions, and maintenance approaches that can lead to more efficient and effective asset management. Prioritizing Safety and Workforce Development: The event emphasizes the importance of safe work practices in high-voltage environments and supports the ongoing professional development of the workforce, ensuring they are prepared for the evolving demands of the power industry.
